## Design Context

### Users
BitCam is used by Minecraft players on modded clients who want to stream a live camera feed above their in-game character without leaving the game. The settings screen is a quick in-session tool: pick a camera, verify the bubble placement, and get back to play fast.

### Brand Personality
Pragmatic, familiar, integrated. The interface should feel like it belongs beside PlasmoVoice and other polished utility mods: direct, low-friction, and confident without looking generic.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a restrained in-game overlay style with dark translucent chrome, flat controls, and a preview that mirrors the actual in-world result. The reference direction is PlasmoVoice's settings shell, but the BitCam screen should stay focused on camera setup and bubble placement rather than decorative panels.

### Design Principles
1. Keep the world visible behind the UI so settings still feel connected to gameplay.
2. Make the preview represent the real in-world bubble placement, not an abstract isolated render.
3. Put controls in a stable, efficient left column and keep the preview stage visually separate.
4. Prefer flat, purposeful chrome over heavy cards or ornamental effects.
5. Match Fabric and NeoForge behavior and visual rhythm closely.
